01 The Operational Problem

The same four failures show up across lean property teams.

01 / Scattered

Messages live in four places.

Resident requests hit email, text, web forms, and portal flows without a working operating view.

02 / Incomplete

Maintenance arrives half-described.

Requests show up missing photos, access details, urgency context, or scheduling information.

03 / Buried

Urgent issues get buried.

Leaks, lockouts, HVAC outages, pests, and safety issues compete with routine resident traffic.

04 / Repeated

Staff repeat the same work.

Coordinators spend hours answering repetitive questions and cleaning up bad handoffs by hand.

Cleaner maintenance handoffs.

Less repetitive inbox work.

The team spends less time re-answering common questions and translating vague requests by hand.

05 Fit

Built for a specific kind of team.

Best fit

  • Roughly 300 to 1,500 doors under management.
  • Lean operations team juggling resident support and maintenance coordination.
  • Clear friction around response time, message backlog, or request handoffs.
  • Need an operating layer on top of current tools, not a full migration.

Not for

  • Firms expecting a full property management system overhaul.
  • Buyers shopping for a generic AI chatbot.
  • Teams unwilling to define escalation ownership and rollout support.
  • Very small portfolios without enough workflow volume to justify the sprint.

07 FAQ

The questions a serious buyer asks.

Does this replace our PMS?

No. The current positioning is an operating and workflow layer on top of your existing tools.

Can humans still step in?

Yes. The system is built around clear human handoff and escalation rules for sensitive cases.

What channels does it cover first?

Email, web forms, SMS, and portal-adjacent traffic are the focus for the initial rollout.

What should the buyer expect from rollout?

A narrow implementation sprint with weekly review and tuning. Not an open-ended consulting project.
